Why Automattic?

The first time I came across the Automattic website two years ago and read about the company, people, values and products, I thought I was dreaming.
Every single line of the company's creed was exactly what I was looking for.
"Does a similar company really exist on Earth?" I reflected.
I began to do some research, watch YouTube videos of Happiness Engineers and other Automatticians, read a lot of contents on Matt Mullenweg's Distributed blog and all this left a great impression on me.
Automattic has been one of the reasons that led me to enroll in a Front end and WordPress development course with Skillcrush: I was willing to achieve the necessary qualifications to be able to apply.

Why now?

It's been a year now since I began the Front end and WordPress development courses with Skillcrush.
Am I job ready? I would really like to know this and I think the only way could be getting a job and trying to apply what I've learned!
I've recently read a quote by Benjamin Franklin that I believe could be applicable to this case:

"Tell me and I forget, teach me and I may remember, involve me and I learn."

My curiosity and desire to learn are big and I can't wait to acquire new skills from other Automatticians.

Why me?

I've enjoyed tinkering with computers and videogames since I was a kid.
After 20+ years in the translation industry, I decided to brush up my technology skills and started learning to code with Skillcrush, an online coding school.
My love for development started with a great passion for blogs: I love reading posts on various contents (meditation, personal growth, running, sea and lighthouses, dogs, etc.) and this made me very curious about creating these websites.
It's been very hard to learn coding alone, starting from scratch, but it was worth it.
It increased my problem-solving ability (I'm a mother of two girls....problem-solving is my daily bread and butter!).
I've worked very hard this year to meet my goals and I'm proud of my achievements.
I've started contributing to WordPress with the Italian Polyglots team and I've had the opportunity to see what is like to work on such a wonderful open source project.
I hope I can contribute further!
